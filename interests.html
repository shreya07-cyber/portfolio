I'm a CS student — but also someone who reads about behavioural finance, gets lost in travel planning, learns guitar at midnight, and crochets while thinking through system designs. Here's a little of what makes me, me.

01

What I'm Into

📈 Finance
Money & Markets

I'm fascinated by how money actually works — behavioural finance, investing psychology, fintech innovation, and why people make irrational financial decisions. My SmartSpend project came directly from this obsession. I believe financial literacy should be accessible to everyone, not just the privileged.

Behavioural Finance Investing Fintech Economics
🧠 Psychology
How People Think

The human mind genuinely puzzles me. I'm drawn to cognitive biases, decision-making, mental health awareness, and the psychology of habits. My EmoCare project — a mood-tracking mental health app — came from a belief that technology can make emotional wellbeing more accessible.

Cognitive Biases Mental Health Habit Formation Decision Making
🌍 Travel
Wanderlust & Wonder

Growing up across multiple Kendriya Vidyalaya schools across India gave me an early taste for new places, new people, new perspectives. Travel isn't just about destinations — it's about realising how differently the world can be organised, and coming back with bigger questions.

Cultural Immersion Solo Travel Pan-India Offbeat Routes
🎸 Music
Guitar

I picked up guitar because I wanted something that had nothing to do with a screen. There's something deeply satisfying about the physical feedback of strings, the slow progress of muscle memory, and how a few chords can completely shift your mood. Still learning — and loving every stumble.

Acoustic Self-Taught Chord Progressions Fingerpicking
🧶 Craft
Crocheting

Yes — I crochet. And I think it's one of the most meditative, pattern-driven, quietly satisfying things I do. There's a surprising overlap with programming: you follow a pattern, debug your mistakes row by row, and end up with something tangible you made with your hands. My friends get handmade gifts.

I regularly fall into Wikipedia spirals at 2am. Current obsessions include how metro systems are engineered, the history of currencies, why some languages have no word for blue, and how compounding works in everything from money to skill-building. Questions excite me more than answers.

02

Things I Believe

01
Good technology should solve real problems, not create new ones. Every project I've built — from parking systems to railway safety to pharmacy trackers — started with a real-world friction point. Code for its own sake bores me.
02
Financial literacy is a form of freedom. Most people aren't taught how money works — and that gap creates inequality. I'm building toward work that bridges technology and financial inclusion.
03
The best ideas come from crossing disciplines. Psychology helped me think about UX. Finance helped me think about algorithms. Guitar taught me how to debug slowly. Crocheting taught me patience with nested loops.
04
Being a beginner at something new is a superpower. Learning guitar with zero music background reminded me what it feels like to be genuinely bad at something and improve anyway. That feeling is important to keep alive as a developer.
05
Curiosity compounds — just like interest. One rabbit hole leads to the next. The more I learn across different fields, the more connections I find, and the more interesting the next question becomes.
